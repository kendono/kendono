# Japan Kanji Division

| Jinmeiyo Kanji |         人名用漢字 |

---

## Kyoiku Kanji

In a broad sense, there are two types of Kyoiku Kanji.

- Kyoiku Kanji for Elementary School (1026 characters)
	- 1st grade (80 characters)
	- 2nd grade (160 characters)
	- 3rd grade (200 characters)
	- 4th grade (202 characters)
	- 5th grade (193 characters)
	- 6th grade (191 characters)
- Kyoiku Kanji for Secondary School & High School (1110 characters)

But commonly, if someone says 'Kyoiku Kanji',
it usually means 'Kyoiku Kanji for Elementary School',
which has '1026 characters' in total.

## Joyo Kanji

Joyo Kanji has 2136 characters.

And that is same amount of Kyoiku Kanji in a broad sense.

But if I say 'Joyo Kanji',
it means 'Kyoiku Kanji for Secondary School & High School',
which has '1110 characters' in total.

## Hyogai Kanji

Hyogai Kanji is a collection of less frequent characters.

The official amount is 1022 characters.

However, 154 characters were included by Joyo Kanji.

And the rest is 868 characters.

So, if I say Hyogai Kanji,
it means that the character belongs to the 868 characters.

## Jinmeiyo Kanji

Jinmeiyo Kanji is a collection of characters for people's name.

Jinmeiyo Kanji has 863 characters.

---

Basically, Japan defines those 4 types of Kanji.

- Kyoiku
- Joyo
- Hyogai
- Jinmeiyo

The rest can be divided in two groups.

- Alternative
- Not Assigned

Let me explain these two types.

---
## Alternative

Alternative means this:

- That glyph does not directly belong to one of 4 types
- But it is an alternative form of one of those characters

For example, China's Simplified Form or
Korea/Taiwan's Traditional Form
might not be one of Japan's Kanji.

However, if it actually indicate the same meaning,
it might be classified as Alternative.

## Not Assigned

'Not Assigned' means this:

- That glyph is not one of Japan Kanji
- And it is not even an alternative form of Japan Kanji

So, from Japan's perspective, it is a quite alien character.
